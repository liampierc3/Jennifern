üíß How to water it perfectly

The Golden Rule:

Unlike your drought-tolerant snake plants, asparagus ferns prefer consistently moist (but not soggy) soil. Think of it as the "Goldilocks" of watering - not too dry, not too wet, but just right!

Frequency:

Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. This might be every few days to once a week, depending on your home's humidity and temperature.

Plant Signals:

If the plant starts to yellow or drop its "needles," it might be a sign of underwatering. If it's getting mushy or developing brown spots, you may be overwatering.
